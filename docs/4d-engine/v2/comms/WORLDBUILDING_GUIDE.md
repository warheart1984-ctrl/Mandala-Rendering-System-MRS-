## 1. Core premise

In this universe, **space has a fourth axis (W)** that characters and cameras can treat as weather, memory, politics, or geometry — depending on the story. Hosts (phones, theaters, game engines) only ever see a **policy-chosen slice** of that space. That policy is an **Observation Mode**: a lens with rules, not a random filter pack.

Writers ask: *What does the world hide until the Mode changes?*  
Directors ask: *What does the audience feel when the lens locks?*

## 2. Vocabulary (story-facing)

| Term | Story meaning | Avoid in dialogue unless earned |
| --- | --- | --- |
| W | The unseen depth; season; secret room | Jargon dumps |
| Observation Mode | Who is allowed to see which cut of reality | “Shader preset” |
| Projection | The handoff from full world → visible stage | “Compression artifact” as plot unless intentional |
| Lineage | Receipt of how the image was chosen — useful for mystery/procedural plots | Fake UI claiming measured ms |
| Host | The 3D stage (city, ship, UI) that cannot compute the full 4D truth alone | “The engine is production-ready” |

## 3. World archetypes (starter kit)

Expand or rename freely; keep Mode continuity across episodes.

| Id | Dramatic function | Sensory palette | Story trap to avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **SLICE** | Revelation through a thin cut | Membrane light, hard silhouettes | Instant portal goo with no rule |
| **DRIFT** | Time / memory parallax | Soft stacked layers, delayed echoes | Claiming live 4–6 ms tech |
| **FOLD** | Conflict as geometric crease | Scaffolding, hyper-box edges as set | “World’s first” title cards |
| **HOST** | Landing in ordinary 3D power | Austere docks, HUD lineage glyphs | “Seamless Unreal” marketing VO |
| **ORBIT** | Relationships rotating in SO(4) metaphor | Paired characters who never share a Mode | Pseudomath lectures |

---

## 4. Character lenses

Give major characters a **default Mode** and a **forbidden Mode**.

| Archetype | Default Mode | Forbidden Mode | Arc seed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cartographer | SLICE | FOLD | Maps lie when W weather shifts |
| Smuggler | DRIFT | HOST | Hides cargo in unseen depth |
| Magistrate | HOST | ORBIT | Enforces which lenses are legal |
| Child / witness | ORBIT | SLICE | Sees bonds adults legislate away |

---

## 5. Scene grammar (directing)

1. **Establish the Mode** in the first ten seconds (light, sound, caption, or character line).  
2. **One Mode change per act beat** unless chaos is the point — then show the cost.  
3. Always stage one clear **4D → 3D handoff** when the plot depends on projection.  
4. If you show accel structures or HUD, caption them as **schematic** when they are explanatory, not diegetic.  
5. Silence is a valid score choice when a Mode locks.

Technical art: Observation Mode RFC is **declared** — use ids consistently with [`../observation/OBSERVATION_MODE_RFC.md`](../observation/OBSERVATION_MODE_RFC.md) when tying to tools.

## 6. Themes that fit the tech metaphor (without overclaiming)

| Theme | How W helps | Soft landing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Censorship | Illegal Modes | Hosts enforce projection policy |
| Grief | DRIFT layers of the same room | Not a product demo |
| Empire | Who owns the lineage receipts | No fake SLA supers |
| Love | Shared ORBIT vs forced HOST | Keep math poetic, light |

---

## 7. Episode / short-film skeleton

1. Ordinary HOST life.  
2. Crack in the membrane (SLICE).  
3. Cost of seeing (DRIFT or FOLD).  
4. Political or personal choice about which Mode becomes law.  
5. Final image: same place, different lens — audience understands the rule.

Companion shot language: [`MULTI_WORLD_CINEMATIC_BIBLE.md`](./MULTI_WORLD_CINEMATIC_BIBLE.md).

## 8. Production honesty box (pin on the writers’ room wall)

- Runnable reference today: **browser / Canvas demo** — caption as partial/reference when used as plate.  
- Unity / Unreal adapters: **skeleton** — do not promise finished DCC pipelines in loglines.  
- FourDRenderer v2: **declared RFCs** — good for lore consistency, not for “ships next quarter” unless evidence exists.  
- Performance numbers in fiction: invent diegetic units; do not paste “4–6 ms” as real engine proof.
